Weekly Diary
Week 1
March 6, 2023

We were assigned to the IT department of Sacred Heart Galimuyod, and our first task was to clean a system unit which we're going to use to reformat and reinstall its OS.

We were also then told to try taking the parts of the PC out and try installing them back in. We were left alone so having experience building a PC unit from scratch before, I assisted my classmates in what the parts are and taught them the parts and where they will be connected/installed.

After our lunch break, we were also assigned to troubleshoot a PC unit sent to the IT department. It wouldn't turn on at first but we eventually figured out that the PSU was the problem, so we replaced it with a different one then it booted up.

March 7, 2023

Our first task of the day in the IT Department was to check the PC units stashed at the office. We first tried testing them to see if it will boot and if it doesn't, we will troubleshoot it with the guidance of the IT personnel.

We managed to test 5 working system units and 2 defective ones, which respectively, have a faulty SSD and don't have a hard drive. I made one mistake by inserting the front panel connectors on the wrong spot as the motherboard didn't have guides and I don't have them memorized.

Afterwards, we needed to recover files from a formatted flash drive, and with the use of simple and intuitive software, the files were recovered.

When we came back after our lunch break, we were then assigned to assemble a LAN ethernet cable, which was hard for me as I was colorblind but my classmate assisted me with the colors. I wasted one RJ-45 connector because I was misinformed on how to lock it using the crimping plier but successfully made my second attempt work.

After finishing our ethernet cables, we tested them using a tester and tried connecting our cable which was a straight-through pattern, and it worked when connected from the router to the PC unit. We also tested the other pair's cable which used the cross-over method, and was taught how to set up a local server from PC to PC in order to store or share files in the other PC and vice versa.
